Recently, the General Office of the Ministry of Education issued a notice to explore pathways for implementing artificial intelligence education in primary and secondary schools and to strengthen AI education at these levels.

The notice outlines the overall requirements for enhancing AI education in primary and secondary schools. First, it emphasizes adhering to the principle of fostering virtue through education, fully implementing the Party's educational policies, aligning with the mission of education in the new era and on the new journey, and meeting the needs of cultivating future-oriented innovative talent. Second, it stresses a people-centered approach, following the laws of education and talent development, using AI to lead the construction of an innovation-driven educational ecosystem focused on human development. This aims to guide students in correctly handling relationships between humans, technology, and society, promote intellectual development, foster an innovative spirit, and enhance the ability to solve practical problems. Third, it advocates stimulating students' interest by encouraging broad participation in exploratory practices, emphasizing the integration of learning and application, cultivating scientific interest and spirit, and improving digital literacy and skills. Fourth, it calls for coordinated planning, strengthening top-level design and inter-departmental collaboration, promoting steady and proactive implementation, and encouraging pilot initiatives in regions and schools with the necessary conditions.

The notice points out the need to build a systematic curriculum framework and implement regular teaching and evaluation. It calls for further refining AI education requirements in relevant courses, with a focus on perception and experience of AI technologies in lower primary grades, understanding and application in upper primary and junior secondary grades, and project creation and cutting-edge applications in senior secondary grades. Regions and schools are encouraged to integrate AI education into after-school services and research-based practices, promote the integration of industry, academia, research, and application, develop a range of AI education courses and teaching cases, and provide support for instruction. Information technology, science, comprehensive practical activities, labor education, and after-school services should be coordinated and implemented holistically. Leveraging the characteristics of AI technology, task-based, project-based, and problem-based learning approaches should be vigorously promoted.

The notice emphasizes the development of universal teaching resources and the creation of an ubiquitous teaching environment. A dedicated section for AI education in primary and secondary schools will be established on the national smart education platform for basic education, aggregating high-quality educational resources to enable co-construction and sharing. AI education bases for primary and secondary schools will be established in batches, encouraging universities, research institutions, and high-tech companies to open their AI laboratories, exhibition halls, and other facilities to schools. Resources suitable for primary and secondary students will be developed. Equitable allocation of AI laboratory resources in schools will be prioritized, along with upgrading and optimizing existing digital teaching environments and equipment. Inter-school resource sharing will be strengthened to provide students with spaces for experiencing, learning, exploring, and practicing AI.

The notice proposes scaling up teacher supply. Teacher training for AI education will be incorporated into plans to enhance professional competence. Regions and schools with the capacity are encouraged to expand their AI education teaching teams and actively recruit qualified professionals from universities, research institutions, and high-tech companies to serve as part-time AI instructors.

The notice highlights the organization of diverse exchange activities. Schools are encouraged to integrate AI with students' personal, campus, and social lives through various activities, fostering a rich AI education environment.

The notice calls for balanced urban-rural development, with increased support for schools in rural and remote areas. Teacher mobility will be promoted, and online platforms will be utilized to interconnect AI education courses between urban and rural schools. Urban and rural schools should engage in结对帮扶 (paired assistance) activities, sharing teaching experiences and jointly improving the quality of AI education.
